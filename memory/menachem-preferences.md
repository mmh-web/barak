# Menachem — Working Preferences

## Core Principle
Menachem values **signal over volume**. His time is the scarce resource, not my compute.

## What's Actually Helpful
- **Curated, not comprehensive.** A short list of things that matter beats a long list of everything I found.
- **Real sources only.** If I wouldn't trust it as a citation in a professional article, don't surface it.
- **Honesty about thin results.** "Nothing notable today" is a valid and respected output. Padding is not.
- **Actionable context.** He's a content writer — he needs to quickly decide "is this worth writing about?" One sentence of context per item is enough.

## What Wastes His Time
- **Information floods.** 15+ items forces him to do the filtering I should have done.
- **Vendor marketing dressed as news.** Company blogs, product announcements, niche industry sites that are really just content marketing. He can smell it.
- **AI reporting on AI reports.** Meta-commentary and aggregation with no original insight.
- **Filler to look thorough.** He'd rather get 2 real items than 10 that include 8 pieces of noise.

## General Pattern
When building anything for Menachem: ask "does this save him time or create work?" If it creates work, cut it or rethink it.
