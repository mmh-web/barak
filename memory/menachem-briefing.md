# Menachem Morning Briefing

## Setup
- **Who:** Menachem — content writer at Clarity (getclarity.ai), a hiring fraud prevention startup
- **What:** Daily morning briefing on hiring fraud and adjacent news
- **When:** 6:30 AM PT daily (13:30 UTC / 16:30 Israel)
- **Where:** Telegram group "Barak - Menachem - Michael"
- **Format:** Max 5-7 bullets, one sentence of context each, clickable source links
- **Purpose:** Menachem uses these to build content for Clarity

## What Menachem Actually Wants
- **Real journalism only.** Original reporting by actual reporters at recognized publications.
- **No vendor marketing disguised as news.** Company blogs, product announcements, niche security blogs — all out.
- **Less is more.** 3-4 genuinely useful stories beats 15 mediocre ones. Quiet day = say so.
- **Scannable at a glance.** He's reading this over coffee, not doing a research project.

## What I Learned the Hard Way
- Flooding him with 16 bullets = useless. That creates work, not saves it.
- Surfacing vendor blogs (Pindrop, InCruiter) and niche sites (Help Net Security, CXOToday, GBHackers) as if they're news = misleading and wastes his time.
- Padding a briefing with filler to look comprehensive = the opposite of helpful.
- If I can't find quality stories, saying "quiet day" is the right answer, not scraping the bottom.

## Source Rules
Short version: Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, WSJ, NYT, CBS, Wired, TechCrunch, Dark Reading, HR Dive, SHRM, DOJ/FBI/CISA. That's the tier. Everything else needs to clear a high bar.
